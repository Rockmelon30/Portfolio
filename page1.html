Pawsitive Vibes

Dog Emotion Image Classifier

August 22nd, 2024 Corey Duncan

Brief Summary

Pets, especially dogs, have become an integral part of social media, often capturing attention through their expressive and endearing behaviors. Many pet owners wish they could better understand their pets' emotional states.

The goal of this project was to develop a machine learning model capable of accurately classifying dog emotions from images. This system has potential applications in enhancing social media filters and features, enabling platforms to identify and respond to specific canine emotions, thus increasing user engagement and interaction. It could also have practical use in understanding the emotional states of rescue dogs to assist in their care.

This project leverages neural networks to build a model that processes images of dogs and classifies them into one of four emotion categories: angry, happy, relaxed, or sad. I started with a dataset from Kaggle containing 4,000 images, equally distributed among these emotional states.

Throughout the project, I experimented with different convolutional neural network (CNN) architectures, various optimizers, data augmentation techniques, and pre-trained models. After extensive testing, I found that the pre-trained VGG16 model yielded the best results.

The model demonstrated strong performance in classifying angry and happy emotions, though it encountered more difficulty distinguishing between the more subtle differences in sad and relaxed images. Interestingly, the model also performed reasonably well when applied to other mammals, such as cats. I believe that expanding the dataset would further enhance the model's accuracy.

Ultimately, my model achieved an accuracy of 86%. Try it out below!
